Cybersecurity

By Bennett Hancock and Charlie Hepperlen

What is cybersecurity?

As defined by Merriam-Webster, cybersecurity is "measures taken to protect a computer or computer system (as on the Internet) against unauthorized access or attack." Essentially, trying to keep out the bad guys.

Why is cybersecurity so important?

There are so many reasons why cybersecurity is getting more important. For one, the world is getting more and more integrated with the internet. Even devices such as toasters and washing machines can connect to the internet, and often have to. Our homes are getting controlled by the internet, our work revolves around the internet. We intentionally put our lives on the internet for peer validation. Money, stocks, health, all performed a good deal online. And as we have gotten more connected, we have added vulnerability.

Incidents

As we have more vulnerability, we have had many problems in the past, even with massive corporations which should know better. One common, repetive example is Meta, formerly Facebook. There have been several concerningly large in scale breaches of user privacy over the years. The Cambridge Analytica scandal, many people's passwords stored completely unsecurely, generally just people's data on public Amazon Web Servers. Credit reporting companies having people's very sensitive private info leaked. There is a good chance that you have had some sort of sensitive info leaked in a breach, but you likely don't know even. Even legally companies actively sell data collected on you. Detailed assumptions about you for better advertising. That is exactly how Google makes money, basically every social media company in existence, etc.

What can you do?

As an individual, there is a surprising amount you can do to improve your own security and privacy. Always use a VPN, use a service to find if you've had a breach and your data has been exposed, keep sensitive info from as many companies as you can, minimize the use of social media, use a password manager that automatically generates and stores very secure passwords, and use an ad blocker. If you really want to get into securing your data, you can use a more privacy focused software selection such as using Linux, cryptocurrency, replacing chrome with something like TOR or Waterfox, and so many more things. Also, I can not stress this enough, stop reusing your passwords and stop making ones that just fit minimum criteria!

What can we all do?

In order to try and alleviate some of the issue, we can put a focus toward cybersecurity in schools. We currently do very little training on really keeping yourself safe online, and a lot of people are paying the price for it. People need to be kept up to date on this stuff, considering how much our lives integrate the internet. Perhaps a mandatory internet safety 101 course, as well as optional additional courses on the subject. We could actually hold corporations accountable for what they do and make regulations on our data, there is so much we can do! We just need to actually do it.
