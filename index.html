Bangkok, Thailand | 13.45° N, 100.29° E

Ending Point

Bangkok is quite the city to say the least. It is the largest city in Thailand by orders of magnitude, the capital, and naturally the economic center of Thailand. The streets are strong here, with many relying on street vendors for their basic necessities.

Environment

Amsterdam

Amsterdam is heavily influenced by its proximity to the ocean and origin as a dam for a river, which is where the name is from. It has a nice, mild oceanic climate with frequent cloudy days. Boat-accessible canals run throughout the city and lead out into open waters. The coolest thing besides the weather is the nearby manmade forest, with the animals and lush greenery of the region within.

Bangkok

Bangkok is far to the southeast and also located on a river, though is at a delta a good way inland from the gulf. Its tropical weather is far warmer than that of Amsterdam, though also wetter. As the original land around Bangkok was a swamp, the ground is composed mostly of a soft clay. This clay presents challenges for urban development, as structures sink into it slowly. A bit more of an issue is that of flooding, which Bangkok is quite prone to due to poor planning and low elevation, often below sea level.

Culture

Linguistics

In Thailand, the predominantly spoken language is Thai. Some useful translations to know are:

English Thai
Hello sa wat dii
Goodbye sa wat dii (Same as hello!)
I don't understand mai khao jai
Do you speak English? puut pasaa angrit dai mai?

Economics

The primary currency of Thailand is the Thai Baht. Its exchange rate in the format of THB:USD is ~36.96:1

Entertainment

Music

Music has an interesting history in Thailand, with jazz being quite popular to the point of former King Bhumibol Adulyadej being a jazz musician and composer. Another genre popular with the upper classes in particular is the combination of western classical and Thai music known as luk grung.

Sportsball

Football (soccer) is a fairly popular sport, with there being a pretty good Thai national team. Unfortunately, Thailand is tainted with the bourgeois scourge known as normal golf. There are a lot of respectable courses around the country.

Food

Thai cuisine is quite a sight to behold and a pain (in the fun spicy way) to eat. Popular dishes include:

  • A Thai dish known around the world and beloved by many is pad thai. It consists typically of rice noodles, shrimp, peanuts, scrambled egg, and any array of vegetables, made in a stir-fry.
  • Another dish is khao phat, a type of fried rice made with jasmine rice and often some form of meat, egg, onions, garlic, and less commonly tomatoes. Often seasoned with all sorts of amazing things.
  • Khao soi is a soup adjacent dish made with a curry-like sauce containing coconut milk, a combination of both deep fried and boiled egg noodles, pickled mustard greens, lime, shallots, and fried ground chilies of course.

These dishes can often be found at the ever-present street vendors, with very good pricing especially for what you get.

Religion

The vast majority of the Thai people practice Theravada Buddhism. The next largest religion is Islam, with less than 10% of the population practicing it.

Important Customs

Feet are not looked upon particularly well by the Thai people. Do not do any of the following with your feet:

  • Point any part of them at someone.
  • Show anyone your sole.
  • Put them on furniture.
  • Move anything with them.

In contrast to feet being looked on with disdain, the head is considered sacred, and you shouldn't touch or pass anything over people's heads. Unrelated,it is good to pass and receive objects using your right hand, and as in America it is rude to point at people, especially with your index finger.

Events

Bangkok has quite a few events, though recently official pride parades have been held and will allegedly continue to be held. It does definitely sound interesting and fun to attend pride events outside of the context of western culture.
